What This Investigation Covers

Prompt comparison is the most common eval task — and one of the most statistically fraught. When you run k comparisons against a baseline (or all-pairs), you need multiple comparison correction or your “best prompt” is probably just the luckiest one.

What you’ll learn

01

Why multiple comparisons inflate false positives — How running k tests at α=0.05 gives you roughly k×0.05 expected false discoveries without correction.

02

Holm correction in practice — How Holm-Bonferroni works step by step, and why it gives more statistical power than plain Bonferroni without increasing false positives.

03

Building CIs for each variant — How to compute smooth bootstrap or Wilson CIs for each prompt and visualize the full uncertainty picture.

04

Reporting honestly — How to say “Prompt C wins” with appropriate hedging, and when the data says “we can’t tell.”
